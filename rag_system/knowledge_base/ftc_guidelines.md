# FTC Endorsement Guides & Creator Compliance

## 16 CFR Part 255: Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials in Advertising

### Key Requirements for Creators

**1. Clear and Conspicuous Disclosure**
Endorsers must clearly disclose material connections (paid sponsorships, free products, affiliate relationships) between themselves and brands. Disclosure must be:
- **Easy to notice** - Not hidden in link text or hashtags
- **Easy to understand** - Use terms like "#ad", "#sponsored", "#partner"
- **Proximate to the claim** - Near the endorsement, not buried below

**2. Material Connection Definition**
A material connection exists when:
- You received payment for endorsing a product
- You received the product for free with expectation of endorsement
- You have an affiliate relationship or commission structure
- There is any other transaction or relationship that affects what you say

**3. Examples of Proper Disclosure**
- Instagram: "#ad Loving this product from @brand"
- TikTok: Use "Brand Partnership" label + "#ad"
- YouTube: Mention in first 30 seconds + video description
- Blog: "SPONSORED" heading + "We were paid/compensated by Brand"

**4. Unacceptable Disclosure Methods**
- ❌ "Thanks to Brand for this amazing product" (no disclosure)
- ❌ "#partner" without #ad (too vague)
- ❌ Disclosure only in comments (too easy to miss)
- ❌ Using only emoji (not clear enough)

### Sector-Specific Rules

**Health & Fitness Claims**
- Must have scientific substantiation for any claims
- Example: "Lose 10 pounds in 30 days" requires clinical studies
- Testimonials alone are NOT sufficient proof
- Work with healthcare professionals for credibility

**Financial Product Endorsements**
- Requires disclosure of "all material terms"
- Restrictions, fees, and risks must be disclosed
- Cannot misrepresent past performance
- Must include disclaimers about future guarantees

**Influencers & Nano-Influencers (Under 10K followers)**
- Rules apply equally regardless of follower count
- No "micro-influencer exception"
- FTC actively monitors smaller creators

### Red Flags in Brand Contracts (FTC Perspective)

❌ **Non-disparagement clause** - Cannot restrict honest opinions
❌ **Requirement to only post positive reviews** - Violates endorsement authenticity
❌ **Prohibition on disclosure** - Illegal per FTC rules
❌ **Post approval delays** - May prevent timely disclosure
❌ **Copyright claim on legitimate disclosure** - Cannot block required disclosures

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- **First violation**: Warning letter (corrective action required)
- **Repeat violations**: Up to $43,280 per violation
- **Criminal penalties**: Up to $10,000 fine for knowing violations
- **Platform action**: Removal from Instagram, YouTube, TikTok
- **Brand liability**: Brands can also be penalized for facilitating non-compliance

### Best Practices

1. **Always disclose** - When in doubt, disclose
2. **Use consistent terms** - Stick to #ad or #sponsored across platforms
3. **Disclose early** - Do it at the beginning, not hidden in the middle
4. **Document agreements** - Keep contracts showing what you disclosed and when
5. **Train yourself** - Stay updated on FTC guidance
6. **Review contracts** - Ensure brand contracts don't restrict required disclosures

### Recent FTC Actions (2023-2024)

- **Influencer crackdown**: $100M+ in settlements from platforms facilitating non-disclosure
- **Amazon Associates**: Must disclose affiliate links on every platform post
- **Course creators**: Cannot use influencer testimonials without disclosure
- **TikTok Shop**: Must disclose sponsored products and commission structure
