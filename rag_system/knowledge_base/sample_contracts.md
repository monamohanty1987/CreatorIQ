# Sample Creator Brand Collaboration Agreements

## Template 1: Standard Single Post Sponsorship (Good Practice)

```
BRAND-CREATOR COLLABORATION AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made as of [DATE] between [BRAND] ("Client")
and [CREATOR NAME] ("Creator").

1. DELIVERABLES
Creator agrees to create and post:
- 1 Instagram post featuring Client's product
- Content must include: product name, price, and unique link
- High-quality photo/video (Creator's standard)
- Posting window: [DATE] ± 2 days

2. COMPENSATION
Client will pay Creator $[AMOUNT] within 10 business days of post publication.
Payment method: [PAYMENT METHOD]
Late payment: 1.5% interest per month after 30 days

3. USAGE RIGHTS
- Creator retains full ownership of the post and all content
- Client may repost to own accounts with photo credit to Creator
- Client may use post for 90 days for marketing purposes
- Extended use (>90 days) requires additional $[X] payment
- Creator content cannot be modified without approval

4. CREATIVE CONTROL
Creator has full creative control over:
- Photo/video styling and execution
- Messaging and tone
- Filter/editing choices
- Caption content (subject to accurate product description)

Brand may request 2 revision rounds. Additional revisions: $150/hour

5. DISCLOSURE
Creator will include: "#ad @brandname" prominently in post caption
Creator will add: "Brand partnership" or "Sponsored" in post description
Placement: First line of caption (not buried below)

6. TERMINATION
Either party may terminate with 7 days' notice if:
- Deliverable hasn't been posted by [DATE + 5 days]
- Client fails to pay by agreed date
- Creator has emergency/force majeure event

7. MORALITY CLAUSE
Both parties agree to professional conduct. Termination allowed if:
- Creator engages in illegal activity
- Brand engages in fraudulent/deceptive practices
- Post promotes illegal products

8. GOVERNING LAW
This agreement governed by [STATE] law
Disputes resolved via mediation before legal action

SIGNED:
Client: ___________________ Date: _______
Creator: ___________________ Date: _______
```

## Template 2: Multi-Post Brand Partnership (Recommended Improvements)

**Good elements:**
- Clear deliverables (posts, stories, reels)
- Specific deadlines and revision limits
- Usage rights clearly defined
- Performance bonuses for engagement
- Creator retains content ownership

**Bad elements to REMOVE:**
- ❌ Perpetual license to content
- ❌ Exclusivity >30 days
- ❌ Unlimited revisions
- ❌ "Creator must maintain positive brand sentiment"
- ❌ Non-disparagement clause preventing honest opinions

**Recommended additions:**
- ✅ Early termination option for either party
- ✅ Clear payment schedule with late penalties
- ✅ Limited territory (e.g., US only)
- ✅ Defined campaign duration
- ✅ Creator approval of final brand edits

## Common Red Flag Clauses Explained

### 1. Perpetual License
**Bad language:**
"Creator grants Client perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use Content in perpetuity."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Brand owns your content forever
- Can use it 10 years later without payment
- Difficult to transition away from brand
- May create conflicts with future partnerships

**Better language:**
"Client may use Content for 12 months from posting date for marketing purposes. Extended use requires additional $[X] payment per year."

### 2. Broad Exclusivity
**Bad language:**
"Creator agrees not to create content for competing brands for 1 year following collaboration."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Blocks most other sponsorships in your niche
- One brand controls your ability to earn
- "Competing" is often undefined

**Better language:**
"Creator agrees not to post content for direct competitors (brands selling identical fitness supplements) for 30 days following this post."

### 3. Unlimited Revisions
**Bad language:**
"Client may request unlimited revisions until satisfied with final deliverable."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Unlimited time investment
- Brand has unrealistic expectations
- Can delay posting indefinitely

**Better language:**
"Client may request up to 2 revision rounds at no cost. Additional revisions billed at $150/hour."

### 4. Overly Broad Morality Clause
**Bad language:**
"Creator agrees not to engage in conduct that could damage Brand's reputation or public perception."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Subjective standard (what "damages reputation"?)
- Applies to your personal life off-platform
- Can be used to void contract for minor issues

**Better language:**
"Creator will not violate local laws or engage in promotion of illegal products. Breach of this clause: Brand may terminate with 48 hours' notice and withhold final payment."

## What Creators Should Always Get

1. **Written contract** - Never handshake deals
2. **Clear payment terms** - Amount, timing, method
3. **Defined deliverables** - Exactly what content, when, where
4. **Usage limitations** - How long brand can use content
5. **Creative control** - What you can/must change
6. **Revision limits** - How many rounds of changes
7. **Termination clause** - Exit options for both parties
8. **Dispute resolution** - Mediation before legal action

## Red Flags That Mean "Negotiate or Walk Away"

🚩 "We don't do written contracts" — Walk away
🚩 Payment >60 days after delivery — Negotiate or decline
🚩 No specified delivery date — Request deadline
🚩 "Perpetual rights to all content" — Negotiate 12-month limit
🚩 >90 day exclusivity — Cap at 30 days
🚩 "Non-disparagement of brand" — Remove or limit to defamation standard
🚩 Unlimited revisions — Cap at 2-3 rounds
🚩 "Creator pays for production costs" — Brand should cover
