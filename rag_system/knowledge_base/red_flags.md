# Contract Red Flag Library for Creators

## CRITICAL Red Flags (Immediate Deal-Killers)

### 🔴 RF-001: Perpetual License Clause
**What it says:**
"Creator grants Brand perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, irrevocable license to use, modify, and distribute Content in all formats and media."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Brand owns your content forever, even after contract ends
- Can reuse 10+ years later without additional payment
- Can modify/repurpose without your approval
- Prevents you from taking content down
- Makes future partnerships difficult (conflicts)

**Financial impact:**
- Content value lost: 50% of deal value ongoing
- Example: $5,000 post but perpetual rights = $2,500+ lost value

**How to fix:**
"Brand may use Content for 12 months from posting date. License expires [DATE]. Extended use requires written agreement and additional payment."

---

### 🔴 RF-002: Exclusive Rights / Extended Exclusivity
**What it says:**
"Creator agrees to exclusive relationship. Cannot post for similar brands for [12+ months]."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Blocks other sponsorship opportunities
- One brand controls your ability to earn
- "Similar brands" is often undefined (entire niche?)
- Can extend beyond contract duration
- May breach existing partnerships

**Financial impact:**
- Lost sponsorships: $500-5,000+ per month
- Example: 5 deals/month × $1,000 = $5,000/month lost

**How to fix:**
"Creator agrees not to post for brands selling identical [product category] for 30 days following this post."

---

### 🔴 RF-003: Morality Clause (Overly Broad)
**What it says:**
"Creator will not engage in any conduct that could damage or reflect negatively on Brand's reputation or image."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- Subjective standard (what "damages" reputation?)
- Applies to your personal life, not just brand content
- Brand can terminate for ANY perceived negative event
- Can be weaponized against you unfairly
- May trigger without your control (outside events)

**Red flag examples:**
- Political post unrelated to brand → Brand terminates
- You attend a controversial event → Contract voided
- News article about you mentions something negative → Grounds for termination
- You date someone controversial → Brand can terminate

**How to fix:**
"Creator will not violate local laws or openly endorse illegal/dangerous products. Brand may terminate if Creator is convicted of felony or engaged in fraud. [90-day notice required]"

---

### 🔴 RF-004: Unlimited Revisions / "Until We're Happy"
**What it says:**
"Creator will make revisions until Brand is completely satisfied with the deliverable."

**Why it's dangerous:**
- No limit on time investment
- Subjective satisfaction standard
- Can delay posting indefinitely
- Brand expectations may be unrealistic
- May conflict with posting timeline

**Example nightmare:**
Brand requests 20 revision rounds over 3 months, then says "Never mind, we don't like the direction" (no payment)

**Financial impact:**
- Time wasted: 50+ hours at no extra pay
- Post delivery delayed: Lost peak engagement period
- Posting deadline missed: Contract voided without payment

**How to fix:**
"Brand may request up to 2 revision rounds at no additional cost. Revisions requested after Round 2: $150/hour. All revisions must be requested within [7 days] of initial delivery."

---

## HIGH Severity Red Flags (Negotiate These)

### 🟠 RF-005: Non-Disparagement Clause
**What it says:**
"Creator agrees not to make negative statements about Brand, its products, or its reputation."

**Why it's problematic:**
- Restricts your honest opinions
- Can prevent you from speaking truthfully about bad experiences
- May violate FTC authenticity requirements
- Could prevent you from warning followers about issues

**Example:**
You experience product failure but contract says you can't post negative reviews → Forced to lie to followers

**How to fix:**
"Creator agrees not to make false or defamatory statements about Brand. Honest product reviews/opinions are permitted."

---

### 🟠 RF-006: No Payment for Unused Content
**What it says:**
"Brand owns all content created. Compensation only if content is published by Creator within [DATE]."

**Why it's problematic:**
- You bear all production risk
- Brand can reject content for any reason
- You've invested time with no guarantee of payment
- No compensation for revisions

**Example:**
You spend 10 hours creating content. Brand rejects it. You get paid $0.

**How to fix:**
"Creator receives [50%] payment upon delivery of content. Remaining [50%] upon publication. Brand may request revisions within [3 days]; if not used within [30 days], full payment still due."

---

### 🟠 RF-007: Late Payment Penalties (Ambiguous or Missing)
**What it says:**
Nothing about what happens if payment is late

**Why it's problematic:**
- Brand can delay payment indefinitely
- You have no recourse
- Can cause cash flow problems
- No incentive for timely payment

**How to fix:**
"Full payment due within 10 business days of content posting/delivery. Late payment: 1.5% monthly interest + $50 late fee per week."

---

### 🟠 RF-008: Broad Non-Compete
**What it says:**
"Creator will not create content for competing brands within [6-12 months] in [undefined territory]."

**Why it's problematic:**
- "Competing brands" is often not defined
- May include entire niche (unfair restriction)
- Can extend to geographies you don't operate in
- Prevents legitimate business opportunities

**How to fix:**
"Creator will not create content for brands selling identical [product category] within [US] for [30 days] following posting date."

---

### 🟠 RF-009: Copyright Claim on Creator Content
**What it says:**
"All content created is work-for-hire. Brand owns all copyrights."

**Why it's problematic:**
- You lose ownership of your own creation
- Cannot use content in portfolio/reel
- Cannot repurpose for other campaigns
- Conflicts with platform policies (TikTok, YouTube, etc.)
- May violate influencer authenticity rules

**How to fix:**
"Creator retains copyright. Brand receives non-exclusive license to use for [12 months] for marketing purposes."

---

### 🟠 RF-010: Undefined Scope of Work
**What it says:**
"Creator will create 'great content' featuring the brand."

**Why it's problematic:**
- No specific deliverables
- Brand has unlimited demands
- Can request anything they want
- Scope creep is inevitable

**Examples:**
- How many posts? 1? 10?
- What quality? Professional photo? Cell phone?
- What platforms? Instagram only? All platforms?
- What messaging? Freedom or scripted?

**How to fix:**
"Creator will deliver: 1 Instagram post (photo + caption, 150+ words), 3 Instagram stories, 1 TikTok video. All must feature [product name] and [price]. Creative direction: [Brand guidelines document]. Delivery: [DATE]."

---

## MEDIUM Severity Red Flags (Review Carefully)

### 🟡 RF-011: Indemnification Clause
**What it says:**
"Creator agrees to indemnify and hold Brand harmless from any claims, damages, or liabilities."

**Why it's concerning:**
- You're responsible for anything that goes wrong
- Covers Brand's own negligence
- Can be very expensive
- Requires liability insurance typically

**How to fix:**
"Creator indemnifies Brand only for claims arising from Creator's breach of this Agreement or illegal conduct by Creator."

---

### 🟡 RF-012: Restrictive Approval Rights
**What it says:**
"All content must be approved by Brand before posting."

**Why it's concerning:**
- Delays posting
- May conflict with platform algorithms (time-sensitive)
- Brand can block content indefinitely
- May prevent timely FTC disclosure

**How to fix:**
"Brand has 48 hours to approve/request revisions. After 48 hours, content may be posted. Revisions requested after posting: Brand cannot block publication but can be noted in comments."

---

### 🟡 RF-013: Undefined Compensation
**What it says:**
"Compensation TBD based on performance" or "Negotiable based on engagement"

**Why it's concerning:**
- No guaranteed payment
- Vague metrics for "performance"
- Creates disputes after content posted
- May not meet minimum wage if tracking hours

**How to fix:**
"Flat fee: $[AMOUNT] due [10 days] after posting, regardless of engagement. Performance bonus: +$[X] per 10K engagements (likes+comments+shares)."

---

### 🟡 RF-014: Governing Law / Jurisdiction
**What it says:**
"This Agreement governed by [Brand's state] law. Disputes heard in [Brand's county]."

**Why it's concerning:**
- Makes legal action very expensive for you
- Puts you at disadvantage (they're local)
- You pay to fly out for disputes
- Their lawyers are local to that court

**How to fix:**
"This Agreement governed by [YOUR state] law. Disputes shall be resolved via mediation in [YOUR city] or through binding arbitration."

---

## LOW Severity Red Flags (Minor Concerns)

### 🟢 RF-015: No Late Payment Penalty Clause
**What it says:**
Nothing specified about late payment

**Impact:**
- Brand can delay payment indefinitely
- Minor: Adds ~30-60 days to payment cycle

**Fix:**
Add: "1.5% monthly interest on overdue invoices after [30 days]."

---

### 🟢 RF-016: Usage Rights Not Specified
**What it says:**
Agreement doesn't mention how long/where Brand can use content

**Impact:**
- Ambiguous but usually defaults to reasonable use
- Minor: You can negotiate later

**Fix:**
"Brand may use Content on [Instagram, TikTok, website] for [12 months] from posting date. Extended use requires permission."

---

### 🟢 RF-017: No Termination Clause
**What it says:**
Agreement has no exit clause

**Impact:**
- Minor: Difficult to exit if relationship goes bad
- Usually falls to default contract law

**Fix:**
"Either party may terminate with [7 days] notice if material breach not cured within [3 days]."

---

## How to Use This Library

**Scoring system:**
- 🔴 Critical flags = Deal-killer (walk away or remove completely)
- 🟠 High flags = Major negotiation needed (don't sign without changes)
- 🟡 Medium flags = Review and fix where possible
- 🟢 Low flags = Nice to have but not deal-breakers

**Your contract red flag score:**
- 0 Critical, 0 High = ✅ Safe to sign
- 0 Critical, 1-2 High = ⚠️ Negotiate before signing
- 1+ Critical = 🚫 Walk away or demand major changes
